Portrait of Inquiry


A final project for fall 2025 semester, CUNY MS Data Visualization Program, prompted by key themes in Critical AI Studies and Data Bias Classes. - Erin Livingston (they/them)

This digital projct is a collection of my personal sources, an interrogation of my outputs using the abilities of scale and pattern recognition of AI. Here I create a portrait of my inquiry from 'primary sources' of my self. This work offers an alternative to the homogeny of contemporary big-box ai models, it offers a deep consideration of senses, desires for information, and the limits of application without materiality.

Prologue

My goal is to consider how one's identity through inquiry could layer with knowledge through senses and material experiences. Could non-text knowledge live in a model? How could I design values-forward? Could I layer a digital system with sources from me? Create an AI 'cyborg' that encourages my curiosity, and values ways of knowing the world around me.

[In the fatigue of a world where we're constantly consuming - and the critical literature doomy stuff I read this semester I want to resist and be the one to feed and to build, to restructure and re-layer the tools around me, imbue them with values they clearly weren't built with.] I desire disclsoure, combined with curiosity in the ways we use AI. This project serves as that, and a deep reflection of the ways I search for, and process, knowledge in myself and others.

This project is composed of three parts.


Dialogic Inquiry

How do we understand ourselves through our interactions with AI? What do my prompts reval of me? Even more interesting to me, how can I wield the answers to create betters versions digital infrasturctures in AI. (and my horizontal digital infrastucture?)


Embodied Inquiry

Art therapy drawings visualizing emotional responses to AI interactions.


Intellectual Inquiry

Conceptual frameworks and ethical considerations surrounding AI.
